About

I am currently pursuing an MSc in Biomedical Engineering at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. My academic background spans environmental science, biomedical engineering, biomaterials, and nanomedicine-related research.

My current research focuses on degradable antibacterial functional films based on gelatin–chitosan composite materials and metal–polyphenol coating strategies for food preservation applications. I am broadly interested in biomaterials, antimicrobial interfaces, nanomedicine, and translational biomedical engineering.

Research Experience

Degradable Antibacterial Metal–Polyphenol-Coated Film for Food Preservation

This project aims to develop biodegradable functional films with antibacterial, antioxidant, and preservation-related properties by integrating gelatin–chitosan film matrices with tannic acid/Zn²⁺ metal–polyphenol networks.

  • Prepared gelatin–chitosan composite films with different formulation conditions.
  • Constructed tannic acid/Zn²⁺ metal–polyphenol functional coatings.
  • Evaluated antibacterial and antioxidant performance using microbiological and chemical assays.
  • Characterized film properties including water contact angle, water vapor transmission, UV-blocking ability, and preservation performance.
  • Analyzed experimental data using GraphPad Prism, Origin, R, Python, and MATLAB.
