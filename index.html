Research Experience

MSc Research Project | Nanyang Technological University, Singapore

Degradable Antibacterial Metal–Polyphenol-Coated Film for Food Preservation

My current MSc research focuses on the development of biodegradable functional films based on gelatin–chitosan composite matrices and tannic acid/Zn²⁺ metal–polyphenol networks for food preservation applications.

  • Prepared gelatin–chitosan composite films and optimized formulation conditions for film formation and stability.
  • Constructed tannic acid/Zn²⁺ metal–polyphenol functional coatings to enhance antibacterial, antioxidant, and preservation-related performance.
  • Evaluated antibacterial and antioxidant properties using microbiological assays and DPPH-based antioxidant testing.
  • Characterized film properties including water contact angle, water vapor transmission, UV-blocking ability, film thickness, and preservation performance.
  • Analyzed experimental data using GraphPad Prism, Origin, R, Python, and MATLAB.
